The basics

What is hire purchase?

Hire purchase (HP) is a type of car finance where the loan is secured against the car itself. You usually pay a deposit — anywhere from 0% to around 20% of the car's price — then make fixed monthly payments for an agreed term, typically between 12 and 60 months.

Throughout the agreement, the finance company technically owns the car and you're the registered keeper. Once you've made every payment, ownership transfers to you automatically — there's no balloon payment and no option-to-purchase fee to find at the end.

Because the car acts as security, HP is often easier to get approved for than an unsecured personal loan, even if your credit history isn't perfect.

Step by step

How does a hire purchase agreement work?

Every HP deal follows roughly the same four stages, regardless of which lender you go with.

1

Choose your deal

Compare HP offers, decide on new or used, and check the representative APR and total amount payable before you apply.

2

Pay an optional deposit

A deposit of around 10% is typical. A larger deposit lowers what you borrow and reduces your monthly payments.

3

Make fixed monthly payments

You'll pay back the amount borrowed plus interest in equal instalments. The finance company owns the car until the final payment.

4

Own the car outright

Once the last payment clears, the car is legally yours — no balloon payment, no further fees, nothing else to decide.

Weigh it up

Pros and cons of hire purchase

HP isn't the right fit for everyone. Here's a balanced look at where it works well and where it doesn't.

Advantages

  • You own the car at the end — with no balloon payment or lump sum to find.
  • No mileage restrictions — HP doesn't cap your annual mileage, unlike some other finance types.
  • Fixed monthly payments — easier to budget for than a variable-rate product.
  • More accessible with poor credit — the car is security, so approval can be easier than a personal loan.

Disadvantages

  • You don't own the car until the final payment — so you can't sell or modify it beforehand.
  • You're responsible for maintenance — as the registered keeper, insurance and upkeep are on you.
  • Repossession risk — falling behind on payments can put the car at risk.
  • Total cost is usually higher — than paying cash, once interest is added.

Representative example & FCA disclosure

Hire purchase is a regulated consumer credit agreement in the UK, overseen by the Financial Conduct Authority (FCA). Lenders are required to show you a representative APR and a full breakdown of the total amount payable before you commit. Always compare the APR, not just the monthly payment, and check for early settlement charges and any option-to-purchase fee — HP shouldn't have one.

Bad credit

Best hire purchase deals for bad credit

HP is one of the more accessible car finance products if your credit history isn't spotless — here's what to expect.

Why HP works for lower credit scores

Because the finance company can repossess the car if you don't keep up payments, HP is seen as lower risk than an unsecured loan — which means some lenders will consider applicants that a bank might turn down. Expect a higher representative APR and possibly a larger deposit request to offset that risk.

Improve your odds

Check your credit report for errors before applying, and correct anything inaccurate — it's one of the fastest ways to improve how lenders see you.

Consider a larger deposit

Putting down more upfront reduces the amount you borrow and can offset a weaker credit profile in a lender's eyes.

Compare more than one lender

Approval criteria vary significantly between lenders — being declined by one doesn't mean you'll be declined by all of them.

FAQs

Hire purchase questions, answered

What is hire purchase (HP) car finance?

Hire purchase (HP) is a type of car finance where you pay an optional deposit followed by fixed monthly instalments. The finance company technically owns the car until your final payment, at which point ownership transfers to you automatically — there's no balloon payment or option-to-purchase fee to pay at the end.

How does a hire purchase agreement work?

You choose a car and agree a deposit, then the finance company pays the dealer and you repay the loan (plus interest) in fixed monthly instalments over an agreed term, typically 12 to 60 months. Once every payment is made, the car is yours to keep.

What deposit do I need for hire purchase?

Most HP deals ask for a deposit of around 10% of the car's price, though some lenders accept 0% deposit while others may ask for more if you have a lower credit score. A larger deposit reduces how much you borrow and lowers your monthly payments.

Can I get hire purchase with bad credit?

Yes. HP is generally considered more accessible than a personal loan if you have a low credit score, because the car itself acts as security for the finance company. You may be offered a higher APR or asked for a larger deposit, so it's worth comparing more than one lender.

Can I get a hire purchase deal on a used car?

Yes, HP is available on both new and used cars, and it's one of the most common ways to finance a used car purchase. Lenders will usually set a maximum age or mileage limit on the vehicle at the end of the agreement term.

Is HP or a personal loan better for me?

A personal loan gives you immediate ownership and more flexibility over the car, but usually comes with higher monthly payments since it's unsecured. HP tends to suit drivers who want the loan secured against the car and are comfortable with the finance company owning it until the final payment.

Can I sell or change my car while it's on HP?

No — legally you cannot sell a car that's on HP until you've made every payment and ownership has transferred to you, because the finance company owns it until then. Selling it without settling the agreement first is a breach of contract.

Can I pay off my hire purchase deal early?

Yes, you can request an early settlement figure from your lender at any time and pay off the remaining balance. Under the Consumer Credit Act you may be entitled to an interest rebate for settling early, though some agreements include a small early settlement charge.

What is voluntary termination on a hire purchase agreement?

Voluntary termination is a statutory right that lets you end an HP agreement early and hand the car back, provided you've paid at least 50% of the total amount payable. You won't own the car, but you won't be liable for the remaining instalments either, subject to the car being in reasonable condition.

What happens if I miss a payment on hire purchase car finance?

Missing a payment can trigger a late fee and a formal notice from your lender, and it may affect your credit file. If you've paid less than a third of the total amount payable, the finance company can usually repossess the car without needing a court order, so it's important to speak to your lender early if you're struggling.
